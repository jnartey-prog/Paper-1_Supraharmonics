Supplementary Information
Title: 
Analytical Framework for Supraharmonic Aggregation Under Spatial Uncertainty Using Point Process Theory
S1 Geometry Scope and Robustness
S1.1 Disc Baseline as Maximum-Entropy Abstraction
Let D⊂R^2 denote a bounded service region of finite area. In the absence of feeder-graph information, the baseline spatial deployment model is a homogeneous PPP of intensity λover D. For analytic tractability, the canonical disc
D=B(0,R)={x∈R^2:∥x∥≤R}
is adopted.
This choice represents a maximum-entropy spatial abstraction subject to two constraints:
	Known service-region size (area ∣D∣).
	Known mean deployment intensity λ.
No claim is made that actual transformer service areas are circular or isotropic. The disc geometry is used solely to enable radial reduction of spatial integrals of the form
∫_D▒〖h(∥x∥)" " dx=2π〗 ∫_0^R▒〖h(r)r" " dr.〗
S1.2 Which Results Depend on Isotropy
It is essential to distinguish analytic structure from integration convenience. The following results do not depend on isotropy or circular geometry:
	Marked shot-noise representation of V(f).
	Campbell–Mecke first-moment expression.
	PGFL-based characteristic function.
	Variance expression
Var[V(f)]=λ∫_D▒E_m [∣g(f,x,m)∣^2]dx.
	Zero-mean cancellation under E[e^jϕ]=0.
	√λscaling law.
	Linear scaling under phase coherence.
Isotropy affects only the reduction of multidimensional integrals to one-dimensional radial form. The stochastic-geometry derivations themselves remain valid on arbitrary bounded domains.
S1.3 Branch-Constrained Surrogate Geometry
To evaluate topology sensitivity, a feeder-constrained spatial surrogate is constructed.
Let the service region consist of Bradial branches of lengths {L_b }_(b=1)^B ┤. Deployment follows a linear PPP with intensity λ_Lper meter. Spatial integrals transform as:
∫_D▒〖(⋅)" " dx〗⟶∑_(b=1)^B▒∫_0^(L_b)▒〖(⋅)" " ds.〗
Under this geometry:
	The marked shot-noise functional remains valid.
	First and second moments follow identical derivations.
	Density-scaling exponents are unchanged.
	Cancellation mechanisms are unaffected.
The only modification is the spatial weighting function inside the variance integral.
S1.4 Geometry Ablation and Empirical Robustness
Geometry sensitivity was quantified via comparison between:
	Disc baseline geometry,
	Star-branch surrogate topology.
Across densities λ=6"-" 18, relative deviations in RMS and percentile metrics remain bounded (see Supplementary Table Sx). Mean deviations are approximately 7%, with worst-bin P99 deviations below 15%.
These results confirm:
	Density scaling exponents are invariant to topology.
	Tail amplification is moderately sensitive but structurally stable.
	Geometry modulates magnitude but does not alter aggregation regime.
S1.5 Interpretation
The disc-based PPP is therefore a mathematically convenient screening model whose role is:
	To provide closed-form integrals,
	To isolate stochastic aggregation mechanisms,
	To avoid overfitting to a specific feeder graph.
Topology modifies spatial weighting but does not change:
	The cancellation mechanism,
	The variance-additivity structure,
	The scaling laws in λ.
Thus, geometry acts as a second-order modulation rather than a structural determinant of supraharmonic aggregation behaviour.
S2 Kernel and Mark Parameter Specification
The analytical framework defined in Sections 2.1–2.8 is instantiated using the deterministic kernel and stochastic mark specifications provided below. These parameters fully determine the propagation model, boundary conditions, and source statistics required to reproduce all numerical results.
Unless otherwise stated:
	Frequency f is expressed in Hz,
	Angular frequency is ω=2πf,
	Spatial distance from the PCC is denoted d≥0.

S2.1 Synthetic Propagation Kernel (Exponential Path)
S2.1.1 Per-Unit-Length Line Parameters
The synthetic propagation model is based on a distributed RLGC transmission-line abstraction with constant per-unit-length parameters:

Parameter	Value

Inductance l	0.45×10^(-3)H/km
Capacitance c	120×10^(-9)F/km
Conductance g	0 S/km

The per-unit-length impedance and admittance are
Z^' (f)=r+jωl,Y^' (f)=g+jωc.
The characteristic impedance and classical propagation constant are
Z_c (f)=√((Z^' (f))/(Y^' (f))),γ_0 (f)=√(Z^' (f)Y^' (f)).

S2.1.3 Transfer Impedance Definition
The frequency- and distance-dependent transfer impedance used in the aggregation kernel is defined as
Z_tr (f,d)=Z_c (f)(Z_L+Z_c (f)tanh⁡(γ(f)d))/(Z_c (f)+Z_L tanh⁡(γ(f)d)).
This expression corresponds to a finite-length distributed line terminated in Z_Land observed at distance dfrom the PCC. It fully specifies the deterministic propagation kernel used in all analytical and Monte Carlo evaluations.

S3 Numerical Scheme and Verification Protocol
This section specifies the numerical procedures used to evaluate the analytical framework developed in Sections 2.1–2.8. The stochastic sampling scheme, deterministic integration policy, and verification criteria are defined explicitly to ensure reproducibility of all reported results.

S3.1 Frequency Grid
All computations are performed across the supraharmonic band
f∈[2,150]" kHz"

using a uniform frequency resolution
Δf=1" kHz".

This produces a grid of 149 discrete frequency bins. All analytical and Monte Carlo evaluations are performed independently for each frequency.

S3.2 Monte Carlo Generation Under the Marked PPP Model
Let D⊂R^2denote the bounded service region with the PCC located at the origin. For each frequency f, a Monte Carlo realisation is generated according to the marked Poisson point process model described in Section 2.

Source Count
The number of active sources is sampled as		N∼"Poisson"(λ∣D∣).

Source Locations
Conditional on N, spatial locations			{x_i }_(i=1)^N ┤ are sampled independently and uniformly over the domain D. For the bounded-disc baseline D=B(0,R), radii are generated as r=R√U,U∼"Uniform"(0,1), which ensures uniform spatial density over the disc.

Electrical Marks
Electrical marks {m_i }_(i=1)^N ┤ are sampled independently from the mark distributions defined in Section S2, including the lognormal magnitude distribution and the specified phase distribution.

Aggregate Voltage Evaluation
For Monte Carlo realisation k, the aggregate PCC voltage is evaluated using the shot-noise representation
V^((k) ) (f)=∑_(i=1)^(N^((k) ))▒g (fⓜ,x_i^((k) )ⓜ,m_i^((k) ) ).

Monte Carlo Estimator
For any functional Ψ(⋅),
E ̂[Ψ(V)]=1/K ∑_(k=1)^K▒Ψ (V^((k) ) (f)).

The estimator converges almost surely to the true expectation as K→∞.

S3.3 Monte Carlo Sample Policies
Three sampling regimes are used.

Default Mode
K=128

Monte Carlo realisations per frequency.

Review-Grade Synthetic Mode
K=2048 realisations per frequency. Unless otherwise stated, all percentile and tail statistics reported in the manuscript use K=2048.


S3.4 Monte Carlo Convergence Diagnostics

To verify adequacy of the Monte Carlo sampling budget, convergence of key distortion metrics was examined as a function of the number of realisations K. Figure S1 shows convergence behaviour for RMS magnitude and the 99th percentile (P99) at a representative reference frequency of 30 kHz. Estimates were computed for K ∈ {256, 512, 1024, 2048}. The shaded regions indicate bootstrap 95% confidence intervals obtained from resampling of the Monte Carlo ensemble. Both metrics stabilise rapidly with increasing K. RMS estimates converge to the full-sample reference value with negligible bias for K ≥ 1024, while the P99 estimator exhibits decreasing confidence width and approaches the full-sample estimate by K ≈ 2048. These results confirm that the review-grade sampling configuration (K = 2048 realisations per frequency) provides numerically stable estimation of both mean-energy metrics and extreme-percentile statistics across the supraharmonic band.

Feeder Benchmark Simulation
For feeder-surrogate benchmarking, K=200 realisations are generated per configuration.

S3.5 Analytical Spatial Integration Policy
Spatial integrals of the form ∫_D▒〖(⋅)" " dx〗 are evaluated using fixed-grid midpoint quadrature with n_"points" =256. No adaptive quadrature is used. The integration resolution is deterministic and identical across all frequencies and scenarios.

S3.6 Random Seed Policy
Deterministic seeds are used to guarantee reproducibility.

Synthetic / Analytical Path
Default seed: "seed"=7

If no override is supplied, the Monte Carlo runner uses this seed.

Feeder Benchmark Path
Global seed:
"RNG_SEED"=20260220.

For each feeder configuration and scenario, the base seed is "seed_base"="RNG_SEED"+"feeder_offset"+97" setting_id"+13" seed_setting".

Individual realisation seeds are "seed_base"+k,k=0,…,K-1.


S3.7 Verification and Benchmarking Structure
Two evaluation stages are performed.

Analytical Verification
Closed-form and semi-closed-form analytical statistics are verified against Monte Carlo realisations generated under identical model assumptions.
Agreement metrics include
	RMS magnitude error
	percentile error statistics.

Model-to-Model Benchmarking
A deterministic feeder-kernel construction is used as a structural comparator. This benchmark is synthetic and model-derived; it does not represent field measurement data.

S3.8 Numerical Agreement Criteria
Internal Robustness Gate
A configuration passes the internal validation gate if 〖"RMS error" 〗_90≤0.10 and 〖"P95 error" 〗_90≤0.10.

Independent Benchmark Gate
Agreement with the independent comparator requires 〖"RMS error" 〗_90≤0.20 and 〖"P95 error" 〗_90≤0.20.

Additional Validation Constraints
The following diagnostic thresholds are applied:
Parameter	Value
Poisson tolerance	0.15
Cancellation factor	0.02
Denominator stability	0.05

Configurations violating these thresholds are flagged during robustness analysis.

S3.9 Scope Clarification
The procedures above provide:
	analytical verification under identical stochastic assumptions, and
	structural benchmarking against a feeder-kernel surrogate.
They do not constitute empirical field validation.

Appendix A
Moment Derivations via Campbell–Mecke Theorem
A.1 Standing Assumptions
Let
Φ ̃={(x_i,m_i)}

denote a marked homogeneous Poisson point process (PPP) on a bounded service region D⊂R^2with intensity λ>0.
Each point carries an independent mark
m_i=(I_i (f),ϕ_i (f),Y_i (f))

representing injected current magnitude, phase, and admittance. Marks are i.i.d. and independent of spatial locations.
For each source (xⓜ,m), define the per-source kernel
g(f,x,m)=(Z_tr (f,∥x∥)" " I(f)" " e^jϕ)/(1+Y(f)" " Z_tr (f,∥x∥)).

The aggregate PCC voltage is the marked shot-noise functional
■(&V(f)=∑_((x_i,m_i)∈Φ ̃)▒〖g(f,〗 x_i,m_i).&&"(A.1)" )

Throughout this appendix we assume the integrability condition
■(&∫_D▒E_m [∣g(f,x,m)∣^p]" " dx<∞,p∈{1,2}.&&"(A.2)" )


A.2 Campbell–Mecke Theorem
Theorem A.1 (Campbell–Mecke).
Let Φ ̃be a marked PPP on Dwith intensity λ.
For any measurable function h:D×M→Csatisfying
∫_D▒E_m [∣h(x,m)∣]" " dx<∞,

the expectation satisfies
■(&E[∑_((x_i,m_i)∈Φ ̃)▒h(x_i,m_i)]=λ∫_D▒E_m [h(x,m)]" " dx.&&"(A.3)" )

A.3 First Moment
Applying Theorem A.1 with h=g,
■(&E[V(f)]=λ∫_D▒E_m [g(f,x,m)]" " dx.&&"(A.4)" )

Under independence of amplitude and phase,
■(&E_m [g(f,x,m)]=(Z_tr (f,∥x∥))/(1+Y(f)Z_tr (f,∥x∥)) " " E[I(f)]" " E[e^(jϕ(f))].&&"(A.5)" )

Phase Diversity
If
■(&E[e^(jϕ(f))]=0,&&"(A.6)" )

then
■(&▭(E[V(f)]=0.)&&"(A.7)" )

Partial Coherence
If
ρ=E[e^jϕ]≠0,

then
■(&E[V(f)]=λρ∫_D▒E_I  [(Z_tr (f,∥x∥)" " I(f))/(1+Y(f)Z_tr (f,∥x∥))]dx.&&"(A.8)" )

Thus the mean grows as O(λ)under phase coherence.

A.4 Second Moment
Expanding the squared magnitude,
■(&∣V(f)∣^2=∑_i▒∣ g_i ∣^2+∑_(i≠j)▒g_i  (g_j ) ‾.&&"(A.9)" )

Diagonal Term
Applying Campbell–Mecke,
■(&E[∑_i▒∣ g_i ∣^2 ]=λ∫_D▒E_m [∣g(f,x,m)∣^2]" " dx.&&"(A.10)" )

Off-Diagonal Term
Using the second factorial moment measure of the PPP,
■(&E[∑_(i≠j)▒g_i  (g_j ) ‾ ]=λ^2 ∫_D▒∫_D▒E_m [g(f,x,m)](E_m [g(f,y,m)]) ‾" " dx" " dy.&&"(A.11)" )

Under phase diversity (A.6), the expectation vanishes, giving
■(&▭(Var[V(f)]=λ∫_D▒E_m [∣g(f,x,m)∣^2]" " dx.)&&"(A.12)" )

Expanding the kernel,
■(&Var[V(f)]=λ∫_D▒E [(∣Z_tr (f,∥x∥)∣^2∣I(f)∣^2)/(∣1+Y(f)Z_tr (f,∥x∥)∣^2 )]dx.&&"(A.13)" )

For the disc domain D=B(0,R),
■(&▭(Var[V(f)]=2πλ∫_0^R▒E [(∣Z_tr (f,r)∣^2∣I(f)∣^2)/(∣1+Y(f)Z_tr (f,r)∣^2 )]r" " dr.)&&"(A.14)" )

Appendix B
Characteristic Function via the PGFL
B.1 PGFL
For a marked PPP,
■(&E[∏_((x_i,m_i)∈Φ ̃)▒u(x_i,m_i)]=exp⁡(-λ∫_D▒( 1-E_m [u(x,m)])dx)&&"(B.1)" )

for measurable uwith ∣u∣≤1.

B.2 Characteristic Function
Let u(x,m)=e^(jθg(f,x,m)). Then
■(&ϕ_(V(f)) (θ)=E[e^(jθV(f))]=exp⁡(λ∫_D▒( E_m [e^(jθg(f,x,m))]-1)dx).&&"(B.2)" )

B.3 Second-Order Expansion
Expanding the exponential,
■(&E_m [e^jθg]=1+jθE_m [g]-θ^2/2 E_m [∣g∣^2]+O(θ^3).&&"(B.3)" )

Under phase diversity (E_m [g]=0),
■(&ϕ_(V(f)) (θ)=exp⁡(-θ^2/2 λ∫_D▒E_m [∣g∣^2]dx+O(θ^3)).&&"(B.4)" )

Thus the second cumulant equals
■(&κ_2=λ∫_D▒E_m [∣g∣^2]dx=Var[V(f)].&&"(B.5)" )

Appendix C
Scaling Laws
C.1 Incoherent Regime
From (A.12),
E[∣V(f)∣^2]=λσ^2 (f)

where
σ^2 (f)=∫_D▒E_m [∣g(f,x,m)∣^2]dx.

Therefore
■(&▭(RMS(f)=σ(f)√λ.)&&"(C.1)" )

C.2 Coherent Regime
If phases are identical,
V(f)=e^jϕ ∑_((x_i,m_i)∈Φ ̃)▒(Z_tr (f,∥x_i∥)I_i (f))/(1+Y(f)Z_tr (f,∥x_i∥)).

The mean becomes
■(&E[V(f)]=e^jϕ λc(f)&&"(C.2)" )

with
c(f)=∫_D▒E_I  [(Z_tr (f,∥x∥)I(f))/(1+Y(f)Z_tr (f,∥x∥))]dx.

Hence
RMS(f)∼O(λ).

C.3 Partial Coherence
Let
ϕ_i=ψ+ε_i,ρ=E[e^(jε_i )].

Then
■(&E[V(f)]=e^jψ ρλc(f).&&"(C.3)" )

The second moment becomes
■(&E[∣V(f)∣^2]=ρ^2 λ^2∣c(f)∣^2+λσ_ρ^2 (f).&&"(C.4)" )

The crossover density is
■(&λ^*=(σ_ρ^2 (f))/(ρ^2∣c(f)∣^2 ).&&"(C.5)" )


Appendix D
Integrability and Convergence
D.1 Finite Domain
If
E[∣I(f)∣^2]<∞

and
(sup⁡)┬(x∈D)∣Z_tr (f,∥x∥)∣<∞,

then the integrability condition (A.2) holds.

D.2 Infinite Domain
For D=R^2, convergence holds if
■(&∣Z_tr (f,d)∣≤Cd^(-α),α>1.&&"(D.1)" )

This ensures convergence of
∫_0^∞▒r^(-2α+1)  dr.

Exponential decay Ce^(-βd)also guarantees convergence.

D.3 Denominator Stability
If there exists c_0>0such that
■(&∣1+Y(f)Z_tr (f,d)∣≥c_0∀d∈[0,R],&&"(D.2)" )
then the kernel remains bounded and singular amplification is prevented.
