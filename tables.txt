From approximately 20–80 kHz:
	RMS stabilises near ~1.0–1.2 V.
	P95 remains near ~2.0–2.3 V.
	Exceedance probability remains in the ~0.09–0.11 range for the defined threshold.
This mid-band plateau aligns with reduced kernel magnitude and consistent √λ scaling behaviour.
3️⃣ Analytical Agreement
The analytical RMS closely matches Monte Carlo RMS:
	Relative RMS errors typically below 1.5%.
	Relative P95 errors typically below 2%.
For example, at 2 kHz:
	Simulated RMS = 1.860 V
	Analytical RMS = 1.838 V
	Relative error ≈ 1.2%.
This confirms high-fidelity reproduction of both second-order and tail statistics under the homogeneous PPP assumption.
Meaningful Deduction
From Table 3 we deduce:
	Frequency dependence of distortion is kernel-dominated rather than density-dominated in this baseline configuration.
	Extreme-percentile distortion (P99) can exceed three times the RMS at low frequencies, highlighting the importance of tail modelling.
	Analytical closed-form expressions provide robust full-band accuracy.
	Low-frequency bins are the primary drivers of compatibility exceedance risk.
Table 3 therefore establishes the homogeneous PPP case as the statistical reference against which clustered and repulsive spatial processes are compared in subsequent sections.

Table G1. Geometry ablation summary for feeder-level distortion metrics
Relative deviation statistics between the baseline feeder geometry and alternative “star–branch” topology across densities (λ = 6–18 units) for three feeder archetypes (149 frequency bins per case).

Technical and Scientific Description
Table G1 quantifies the sensitivity of aggregate distortion metrics to feeder geometry assumptions by comparing the reference configuration against a star–branch topology at multiple active-source densities.
Across densities for Feeder A (urban short):
	RMS mean relative difference: ≈ 6.97–6.98%
	RMS 90th-percentile difference: ≈ 9.2–9.3%
	RMS maximum per-bin difference: ≈ 9.5–9.6%
For extreme-percentile metrics:
	P95 mean relative difference: ≈ 7.0%
	P99 mean relative difference: ≈ 7.2%
	Maximum P99 per-bin deviation: up to ≈ 11.9%
Worst-case discrepancies typically occur in mid-to-high frequency bins (≈70–90 kHz).
Notably, relative deviations are remarkably stable across density levels, indicating that geometry-induced bias is largely independent of λ in this configuration.

Meaningful Deduction for Further Analysis
1️⃣ Geometry introduces systematic but bounded bias.
Mean deviations of ~7% across RMS, P95, and P99 indicate that feeder topology affects distortion magnitude, but the magnitude of the effect remains moderate.
2️⃣ Density scaling is robust to topology.
The near-constant relative error across λ = 6–18 suggests that √λ scaling behaviour is preserved under geometry variation.
3️⃣ Tail metrics are slightly more sensitive.
Maximum P99 deviations exceeding 10% indicate that extreme-percentile distortion is more sensitive to structural feeder assumptions than RMS.
4️⃣ Mid-band frequencies are most sensitive.
Worst-frequency bins (≈70–90 kHz) align with regions where impedance-phase interactions are strongest.

Scientific Significance
This ablation analysis demonstrates that while spatial topology influences distortion magnitude, the stochastic marked-PPP aggregation framework remains structurally stable across geometry variants. The moderate error levels confirm that the analytical formulation captures the dominant physics, with topology acting as a secondary modulation rather than a primary driver.
